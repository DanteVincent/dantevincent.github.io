About me

Welcome! This is Wenqi Chen’s personal blog!

My name is Wenqi Chen, a Ph.D. student from Tsinghua University, P. R. China. The area I am focusing on is the computational kinetics of chemical reactions in the gas phase over a broad temperature and pressure range by using Variational Transition Theory. I am also interested in the anharmonic corrections for large-amplitude motions [such as low frequency out-of-plane bends and internal rotations (torsions)] of complex molecules, which are needed for obtaining accurate partition functions, thermodynamic functions, and/or transition-state-theory rate constants. I’ve been devoting myself to developing new tools for correcting torsional anharmonicity & finding conformers.
